Gabriela Silang

The Fearless Filipina Revolutionary

Biography

Gabriela Silang (March 19, 1731 – September 20, 1763) was the first Filipina to lead a revolt against Spanish colonial forces. After the assassination of her husband Diego Silang, she took up leadership of the resistance in the Ilocos region, becoming a symbol of bravery and defiance.

Born in Ilocos Sur to a mestiza mother and an Ilocano father, Gabriela was raised by a parish priest and educated in Spanish and religion. She married Diego Silang, a revolutionary leader, and became his closest ally in the fight for freedom.

"I will fight to the last breath for my people’s liberty."

— Gabriela Silang

Did You Know?

Gabriela is often called the “Joan of Arc of the Philippines” for her bravery and leadership in battle.

Leadership and Battles

After Diego's death in 1763, Gabriela continued their revolt, organizing troops and launching guerrilla attacks. She led her men in the mountains of Abra and fought valiantly against better-armed Spanish troops.

Despite being heavily outnumbered, her leadership inspired loyalty and courage. She became a threat to Spanish rule in the north.

Capture and Execution

On September 20, 1763, Gabriela Silang was captured along with her loyal followers. She was publicly hanged in Vigan Plaza as a warning to others.

  • Captured after a betrayal by locals
  • Executed at age 32
  • Refused to beg for mercy from the Spanish

Impact

Gabriela’s death did not silence the resistance. Her legacy continued to inspire Filipino women to fight for freedom and justice.

Legacy and Honors

Gabriela Silang remains a national heroine and feminist icon in the Philippines.

  • Monuments in her honor stand in Ilocos and Manila
  • The Gabriela Women's Party was named after her
  • She appears on commemorative stamps and coins
  • Her name is taught in schools nationwide

"Gabriela Silang proved that resistance knows no gender."

Timeline of Gabriela Silang’s Life

  • March 19, 1731: Born in Santa, Ilocos Sur
  • 1762: Married Diego Silang and joined his revolution
  • May 1763: Diego Silang assassinated
  • June–Sept 1763: Led resistance in Abra
  • Sept 20, 1763: Captured and executed by Spanish
  • Modern era: Honored as a national heroine
